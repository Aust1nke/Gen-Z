Why Gen Z Graduates Struggle to Get Hired

The Challenge

Gen Z graduates are among the most educated, yet they face unprecedented challenges in securing jobs. Factors such as lack of professionalism, disrupted learning during the pandemic, and unrealistic expectations from employers are just some of the key barriers.

1. Perceived Lack of Professionalism

Many employers find that Gen Z candidates lack basic workplace etiquette. For example, some have shown up for virtual interviews in casual settings or avoided turning on their cameras. Source

2. Pandemic Disruptions

The COVID-19 pandemic deprived Gen Z of critical networking and internship opportunities, leaving them less prepared for workplace challenges. Source

3. Unrealistic Expectations

Many young graduates demand higher starting salaries and flexible work conditions, which employers often perceive as misaligned with their experience levels. Source

The Underlying Issues

4. Generational Disconnect

Employers cite challenges such as missed deadlines, tardiness, and lack of teamwork among new hires. Mentorship is key to bridging these gaps. Source

5. Mental Health Challenges

Open discussions about mental health, while positive, sometimes come across as a lack of resilience to employers. Source
